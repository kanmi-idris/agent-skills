# Naming Types Reference

A strong naming session explores multiple categories. These are the main naming archetypes and when they work best.

---

## 1. Evocative / Emotional
Names that evoke a feeling instead of literally describing the product.

**Examples:** Notion, Stripe, Drift, Asana, Loom, Slack, Figma

**Best for:** Brands that want to own a feeling.

---

## 2. Invented / Coined Words
Made-up words, portmanteaus, or transformed words that are highly ownable.

**Examples:** Kodak, Xerox, Spotify, Skype, Rolex, Verizon, Hulu

**Best for:** Brands that need strong distinctiveness and global flexibility.

---

## 3. Metaphor / Analogy
Names borrowed from another domain that create a resonant comparison.

**Examples:** Amazon, Apple, Stripe, Firefly, Ribbon

**Best for:** Abstract or ambitious brands that need narrative power.

---

## 4. Founder / Eponymous
Names based on a founder, person, or archetypal authority signal.

**Examples:** Ford, Disney, Gucci, Tesla

**Best for:** Founder-led or premium brands.

---

## 5. Descriptive / Functional
Names that literally describe what the product does.

**Examples:** Basecamp, Zoom, Dropbox, Snapchat, Salesforce

**Best for:** Early-stage clarity.

---

## 6. Experiential / Verb-Friendly
Names that are likely to become verbs or repeated behaviors.

**Examples:** Google, Uber, Zoom, Tweet

**Best for:** Consumer products and network-effect products.

---

## 7. Abstract / Designed
Names with no inherent meaning that rely on sound design and brand building.

**Examples:** Kodak, Accenture, Agilent

**Best for:** Large brands that can invest heavily in meaning creation.

---

## 8. Place / Geographic Reference
Names that borrow from a location or a feeling associated with a place.

**Examples:** Amazon, Patagonia, Atlassian

**Best for:** Brands that want to inherit associations like scale, ruggedness, or global reach.

---

## 9. Number / Symbol
Names built around numbers, punctuation, or symbolic structures.

**Examples:** 3M, 7-Eleven, Web.com

**Best for:** Industrial or technical positioning when used intentionally.

---

## 10. Portmanteau / Mashup
Names formed by combining two words into a new concept-word.

**Examples:** Pinterest, Instagram, YouTube, Microsoft

**Best for:** Brands built on two equally central ideas.

---

## Naming Category Decision Matrix

| If the brand needs to feel... | Consider categories... |
|---|---|
| Bold, powerful, scaled | Metaphor, Invented |
| Warm, human, approachable | Evocative, Experiential |
| Trustworthy, established | Eponymous, Geographic |
| Cutting-edge, technical | Invented, Abstract |
| Simple, clear, accessible | Descriptive, Portmanteau |
| Viral, behavior-changing | Experiential |
